# Heal Internet

*This page is co-authored by the groups working on the Heal Internet challenge: Björnlandet, Stenshuvud, Sonfjället.*

## Problem framing

The challenge "Heal the Internet" is focused on how digital platforms and online systems/services can support healthy, transparent and trustworthy interactions online. Internet plays a central role in our daily life, such as communication, learning, entertainment, but many online environments are shaped by systems that prioritize traffic and growth over user well-being. This has opened up for challenges such as misinformation, targeted ads and less trust in digital spaces.

The small web or healing the internet encourage how technology can be designed to address these challenges. The goal is to create safer and more meaningful online experiences by promoting trustworthy information, healthier communities, transparency, and user-centered design. This challenge invites participants to explore creative ways to improve the quality of online interactions and digital environments.

The challenge also explores the difference between the "big web" and the "small web". While the big web is often shaped by algorithms, advertising, and large-scale engagement. The small web focuses more on communities, creativity and authenticity. Contributing to a healthier internet experience.
